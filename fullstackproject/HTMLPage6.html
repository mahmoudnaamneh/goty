    THE GAME AWARDS HITS 103 MILLION LIVESTREAMS 


     THE GAME AWARDS 2022 DELIVERS HISTORIC 103 MILLION LIVESTREAMS
     The Video Game Industry’s Year-End Showcase Continues Record-Setting Growth For 9th Straight Year, Up 20% Over 2021 Show
     LOS ANGELES (Friday, December 16)  – The Game Awards, the video game industry’s year-end celebration, today announced that
     the ninth annual show shattered viewership records with an estimated 103 million global livestreams, up 20% over 2021’s 
     record-setting performance. With appearances from Al Pacino, Halsey, Daniel Craig, Rian Johnson and Jessica Henwick, Hozier,
     Hideo Kojima, Pedro Pascal and Bella Ramsey, Troy Baker and Ashley Johnson, Keegan Michael-Key, Cameron Monaghan, Fuslie
     and Valkyrae, Animal from The Muppets and more, plus spectacular world premieres of games including FINAL FANTASY XVI,
     STAR WARS Jedi: Survivor, Hades II, Death Stranding 2, JUDAS, and the first clip of the Super Mario Bros. Movie film,
     The Game Awards once again reaffirmed video game’s position as the preeminent force in global entertainment.
